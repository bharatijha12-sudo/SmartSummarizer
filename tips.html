Tips & Psychological Tricks for Effective Study

Use these science-based strategies to focus better, remember more, and score higher.

1. Pomodoro Method

Study for 25 minutes with full focus, then take a 5-minute break. After four cycles, take a longer break (15–20 minutes). This prevents burnout and keeps your brain fresh.

2. Active Recall

Close your book and try to write down or speak out whatever you remember. Testing yourself strengthens your memory much more than just reading or highlighting.

3. Spaced Repetition

Instead of revising the same topic again and again in one day, revise it today, then after 2 days, then after 1 week. This spacing trains your brain to store information long-term.

4. Environment Control

Keep your phone away, turn off unnecessary notifications, and study on a clean desk. Your environment silently controls how much you can focus.

5. Mix Subjects Wisely

Alternate between theory-heavy subjects and problem-solving subjects. This keeps your brain from getting bored and improves overall performance.

6. Sleep & Health

All-night study looks cool but kills your memory. Proper sleep, hydration, and light exercise will help you perform way better in exams.
